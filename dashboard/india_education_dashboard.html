India's Education Progress & Gender Gap

25 years of official World Bank / UNESCO data (2000-2024) on India's literacy, school completion, enrollment parity, and education spending.

A note on the 2023 spike Literacy jumped from 76.3% (2022) to 81.7% (2023) then eased to 78.2% (2024) — this is very likely a survey-methodology revision rather than a genuine one-year swing, since literacy is measured via infrequent surveys, not continuous tracking. The 24-year trend is the reliable signal, not any single year.

Key findings

  • Generational gap Enrollment gender parity (girls vs. boys currently in school) was achieved by ~2012 and has held near or above 1.0 since — but adult literacy gap still reflects older women who missed that access, showing this is a stock problem working through the population, not a current-enrollment problem.
  • Literacy Rose from 61.0% (2001) to 78.2% (2024), a genuine +17.2pp gain over 23 years.
  • Gender gap Narrowed from 25.6 percentage points (2001) to 15.0 (2024) — real progress, but still a substantial gap.
  • Spending Education spending as a share of GDP was essentially flat to slightly declining (4.32% in 2000 vs. 4.10% in 2022) even as outcomes improved — gains likely came from GDP growth and efficiency rather than proportionally higher investment.
  • Primary completion Has hovered near-universal (94-99%) for most of the last 15 years — this metric is largely saturated; lower-secondary completion (55% in 2002 → 87% in 2024) is where the real remaining growth is happening.
